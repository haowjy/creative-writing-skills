# Fantasy Prose Addendum

Fantasy rewards wonder, coherence, texture, and the feeling that the world
extends beyond the page. The invented world should constrain the scene instead
of sitting behind it as exposition.

## Protect

- World details that matter to character choice, cost, status, or danger.
- Terminology introduced through use before explanation when possible.
- Magic and politics with boundaries, costs, and social consequences.
- A sense of history without pausing the scene to lecture.

## Common LLM Failure Modes

- Front-loading lore before the reader needs it.
- Explaining magic as a clean system detached from culture or cost.
- Using invented terms without enough context to orient.
- Making every place sound generically ancient, grand, or mystical.

## During Revision

Check whether each world detail changes the scene pressure. Keep wonder, but
anchor it in action, consequence, and point of view.
