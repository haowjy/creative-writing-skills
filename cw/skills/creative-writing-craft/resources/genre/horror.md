# Horror Prose Addendum

Horror rewards dread, uncertainty, vulnerability, and delayed understanding. The
reader should feel the shape of danger before every mechanism is explained.

## Protect

- Unknowns that make the reader participate.
- Sensory wrongness: small violations before full revelation.
- Pacing that lets anticipation do work.
- Character vulnerability, denial, rationalization, and delayed recognition.

## Common LLM Failure Modes

- Explaining the threat too early.
- Naming fear instead of making the reader infer it from perception and action.
- Escalating every beat until the scene becomes noise.
- Using gothic adjectives as a substitute for concrete wrongness.

## During Revision

Clarify geography and causality enough for fear to land, but do not clarify the
mystery by default. Preserve silence, partial perception, repetition, and
misinterpretation when they create dread.
