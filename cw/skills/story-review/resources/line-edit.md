# Line Edit

Line editing improves how the prose moves while preserving the author's voice.
It operates after the scene or chapter is structurally sound enough to polish.

## Check

- **Voice**: does the language belong to this narrator, POV, character, genre,
  and project style?
- **Rhythm**: do sentence lengths, paragraph breaks, punctuation, and beats vary
  with the emotional pressure?
- **Clarity**: can the reader follow action, reference, geography, and thought
  without extra explanation?
- **Texture**: are details specific and selected, or generic and piled on?
- **Subtext**: does dialogue carry more than surface information?
- **Redundancy**: are emotions, motives, and implications stated after the text
  already made the reader infer them?

## Report

Name recurring line patterns and give a few representative examples. When a
sentence needs changing, explain the intended effect: sharper POV, cleaner
image, stronger rhythm, less explanation, more pressure, clearer referent.

Preserve deliberate roughness, ambiguity, silence, repetition, or broken rhythm
when it serves the voice or scene effect.
