# Proofreading

Proofreading is the final surface pass after development, line editing, and
copyediting. It is not the place to rethink structure or voice unless a surface
error reveals a larger unresolved issue.

## Check

- typos, missing words, duplicated words
- punctuation and capitalization slips
- spacing, indentation, paragraph breaks, chapter headings
- formatting consistency, scene breaks, page/layout artifacts
- wrong names, wrong homophones, accidental autocorrects
- style-sheet drift introduced after copyedit

## Report

Keep the report terse and locatable. Group repeated patterns, but list one-off
proofing errors with enough context to find them. If you notice a developmental
or line issue, put it under a separate “not proofing” note so the author can
choose whether to reopen the draft.
